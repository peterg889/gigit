# Launch Metro Selection — Shortlist

**Date:** June 2026
**Method:** Web research pass against the PRD Phase 0 criteria: (1) no GigFinesse presence, (2) brewery/coffee/bar density, (3) grassroots music + comedy scene, (4) metro population ~500K–3M, (5) musician supply (BLS location quotients), (6) local competition. Hard data vs. promotional claims flagged in §5.

---

## 1. Competitive update that reshapes the criteria

**GigFinesse is bigger than our original research showed.** As of the May 2026 Series A, they claim **50,000+ shows across 30+ cities**, ~1,000 events/month, and have **relocated HQ from NYC to Austin** ([The SaaS News](https://www.thesaasnews.com/news/gigfinesse-raises-15m-series-a/), [PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/277791-31)). Confirmed/evidenced markets: NYC, LA, Chicago, Nashville, Austin (HQ), Dallas, San Antonio, Miami, Denver, Las Vegas, Birmingham AL, DC-area. They do not publish a full market list — "30+" is a company claim.

**The opening:** their post-Series-A motion is visibly upmarket — hotel chains and hospitality groups (St. Regis, Ritz-Carlton, Fairmont, Hard Rock partnerships). The **indie bar/brewery/coffee-shop long tail in mid-size metros** is comparatively open, and comedians + sound techs remain outside their core wedge. This *strengthens* the case for a mid-size-metro, long-tail launch rather than weakening it.

## 2. Ranked shortlist

| Rank | Metro | Pop (2025) | Demand density | Supply (musician LQ) | Competition | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Milwaukee, WI** | 1.58M | **#1 US bars per capita (36.7/100K)**; WI 247 breweries; 100+ concerts/month | 1.06 (adequate) | None — no GigFinesse evidence; 2 comedy clubs for 1.6M = comedy whitespace | Highest bar density in America, Chicago talent spillover 90 min away, zero platform incumbency |
| **2** | **Pittsburgh, PA** | 2.42M | 50+ local breweries; breweries routinely program live acts; top-10 bar-density rankings | **1.48 — highest of all candidates** | None; 1 full-time comedy club for 2.4M; active indie comedy producers | Best working-musician concentration on the list, low cost of operating, comedy badly underserved |
| **3** | **Richmond, VA** | 1.39M | 40+ breweries in immediate vicinity ("few cities with a more impressive brewery-per-capita ratio" — Paste); 20+ live venues incl. brewery stages | 0.87 ⚠ below average | None; 1 comedy club | Demand-side dream in a compact geography; the ⚠ means artist supply needs heavier Phase 0 seeding |
| **4** | **Grand Rapids, MI** | 1.18M | "Beer City USA" 13 straight years; 35+ Ale Trail breweries, 80–100+ metro-wide; taprooms ARE the venue stock | No LQ data ⚠ | **Cleanest competitive field found** | Purest small-venue demand profile in the country; supply-side data gap is the open question |
| **5** | **Portland, OR** | 2.51M | OR 297 breweries (top-7 per capita); 2.47 venues/100K; dozens of weekly open mics | 1.45 | No GigFinesse found; Sofar active; mature DIY/self-organized scene | Huge supply, but the scene's existing self-organization makes it harder to monetize than the Midwest metros |

**Wildcard — Asheville, NC (422K, below the 500K floor):** the single highest breweries-per-capita city in the US (60+ for ~95K city residents), documented musician oversupply, zero platform competition. Too small to anchor liquidity, but a cheap **pilot wedge or market #6**.

### Recommendation
**Milwaukee primary, Pittsburgh close second.** Milwaukee optimizes demand density + competitive vacuum; Pittsburgh optimizes supply density + comedy whitespace. A defensible tiebreaker: run the PRD's 50-venue survey (now executable as the AI prospect-intelligence scrape, per `ai-era-features.md` F-AI.14) in *both* metros and let observed live-programming density decide. Both are also drive-distance from Chicago/Grand Rapids/Richmond-adjacent corridors for market #2–3 expansion.

| Metro | Why excluded |

| Austin | **GigFinesse HQ** + the most saturated live-music labor market in the US (SXSW-distorted) |

## 5. Hard data vs. soft claims

**Hard:** Brewers Association state brewery counts; BLS-derived musician LQs (2021 metro table + Feb 2025 Insurance Canopy pull); MSA populations; GigFinesse funding and Denver partnership PR; New Orleans music-census pay figures.

**Soft (treat as directional):** GigFinesse "30+ cities" (company claim, no published list — Portland/Minneapolis absence is *absence of evidence*, not confirmation); Grand Rapids "100+ venues downtown" (tourism-bureau claim); Milwaukee 36.7 bars/100K (2022 study mixing bars+breweries); Richmond "40+ breweries" (journalistic count); comedy-club counts (comedian-authored survey). BLS blocks automated access, so current-year metro LQs are proxied from 2021/Feb-2025 pulls; BLS also undercounts self-employed gig musicians, which understates supply everywhere (most severely in Asheville/NOLA).

**Decision dependency:** final Milwaukee-vs-Pittsburgh call should come from the Phase 0 prospect-intelligence scrape (count venues actually programming live entertainment from event calendars/Instagram in both metros) — the "% of venues hosting live music" stat that doesn't exist publicly is buildable ourselves in a weekend per metro.
